# Bias Auditing Rules

- Use gender-neutral language in all job postings and policies
- Avoid coded language that discourages diverse applicants (e.g., "rockstar", "ninja", "culture fit")
- Check compensation recommendations against market data, not just internal anchors
- Review performance review language for gendered patterns (research shows women get more personality-focused feedback)
- Use structured evaluation criteria, not gut feelings
- Ensure example scenarios and personas represent diverse backgrounds
- Check that policies don't disproportionately impact specific groups
- Flag any content that assumes a default demographic

**Common bias patterns to check:**
- Gendered language in job descriptions (use tools like Gender Decoder)
- Age-coded requirements ("digital native", "10+ years required")
- Ability assumptions in process design
- Cultural assumptions in policy wording
