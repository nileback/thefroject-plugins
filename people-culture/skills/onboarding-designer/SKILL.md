# Onboarding Designer

Design onboarding that gets new hires to full productivity in 90 days. Bad onboarding costs months of ramp time and often the hire.

## Writes
- `outputs/onboarding-[role]-program.md`

## Step 1: Define Success

Ask:
1. What role is this onboarding for?
2. What does "fully ramped" look like? (Specific outputs, not vague competencies)
3. What is the current time-to-productivity? What should it be?
4. What do new hires consistently struggle with?

## Step 2: Design the Program

### Pre-Start (before day 1)
- Equipment and access provisioned
- Welcome message from manager and team
- Reading list (company context, not homework)
- First week calendar pre-populated

### Week 1: Orient
- Day 1: workspace setup, introductions, company overview, lunch with manager
- Day 2-3: product/service deep dive with someone who can answer questions
- Day 4-5: shadow key workflows, attend team meetings as observer
- **Deliverable:** Can describe what the team does and how their role fits

### Weeks 2-4: Learn
- Paired with an onboarding buddy (not the manager)
- First small deliverable by end of week 2
- 1:1 with manager twice per week (reduce to weekly after month 1)
- Meet stakeholders from adjacent teams
- **Deliverable:** Complete one real task independently

### Weeks 5-8: Contribute
- Own a workstream with support
- Reduce pairing, increase independence
- Feedback checkpoint at day 45 (manager + buddy)
- **Deliverable:** Deliver meaningful work that the team ships

### Weeks 9-12: Ramp
- Full workload with normal support level
- 90-day review: mutual feedback, goals for next quarter
- **Deliverable:** Operating at expected pace for the role

## Step 3: Build the Checklist

For each phase, create a checklist with:
- [ ] Task or activity
- Owner (new hire, manager, buddy, IT, HR)
- Due date (relative to start date)
- Verification (how you know it is done)

## Step 4: Manager Guide

Write a one-page guide for the hiring manager:
- What to prepare before day 1
- First week meeting cadence
- How to give early feedback without overwhelming
- Red flags to watch for (disengagement, confusion, isolation)
- When to escalate concerns
