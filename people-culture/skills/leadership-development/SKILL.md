# Leadership Development

Design programs that grow leaders inside the org — from first-time managers stepping up from IC roles, through senior managers preparing for director, through high-potential ICs being prepared for exec-track succession.

## Gather context

Ask if not provided:

1. **Org size and stage** — under 50, 50-200, 200-500, 500+. Different leadership challenges per stage.
2. **Current state** — formal program already? Ad-hoc? First-time managers thrown in deep?
3. **Pain point** — what triggered the conversation? (Failed promotion, manager-related attrition, scaling team)
4. **Audience** — who's the program for? (All managers, IC-to-manager candidates, senior managers, execs)
5. **Resources** — budget, time per participant, internal vs external trainers, executive sponsorship.

## Three program archetypes

### Archetype 1: First-time manager onboarding (60-90 days)
For ICs being promoted to manage their first team.

Curriculum modules:
1. **From IC to manager** — your job changed. Output is now your team's output. Week 1.
2. **One-on-ones** — cadence, structure, what to discuss. Week 2.
3. **Setting expectations** — explicit goals, role clarity, performance bar. Week 3.
4. **Giving feedback** — radical candor, the SBI model, micro-feedback. Week 4-5.
5. **Hiring** — interview design, scorecards, how to read signal. Week 6-7.
6. **Performance management** — how to address underperformance early. Week 8-9.
7. **Difficult conversations** — termination, denied promotion, bonus disputes. Week 10-12.

Format: 60-90 min sessions weekly, plus a buddy (more senior manager). Mix of frameworks, role-play, and real situations the participant brings.

### Archetype 2: Senior manager / director track (3-6 months)
For managers preparing for director or two-up roles.

Curriculum modules:
1. **Strategy** — connecting team work to company strategy. Diagnosing what to do, not just executing.
2. **Operating cadence** — quarterly planning, OKR design, business reviews.
3. **Org design** — when to hire, when to restructure, span of control.
4. **Cross-functional leadership** — running a team without authority over all the players.
5. **People investment** — coaching managers, succession in your team, talent reviews.
6. **Executive presence** — board/exec communication, presenting up.
7. **Self-management** — managing energy at scale, delegation, calendar audit.

Format: monthly 2-day intensives, plus a senior coach (an exec from inside or outside).

### Archetype 3: Succession planning (ongoing)
For identifying and preparing future execs.

Process:
1. **Talent review** — twice a year, leadership team identifies high-potential individuals.
2. **Development plans** — each high-pot has a written plan with stretch projects, exposure, coaching.
3. **Exposure** — board observation, customer meetings, exec all-hands presentations.
4. **Rotation** — when feasible, cross-functional or geographic moves.
5. **Cooldown / readiness check** — quarterly review of progress.

This is not a curriculum; it's an operating cadence with named individuals.

## Common modules across all three

Every leadership program should include:

- **Self-awareness assessment** — DISC, MBTI, or similar, used as a starting point not a pigeonhole.
- **Coaching skills** — how to ask questions instead of giving answers.
- **Conflict navigation** — see `conflict-resolution`.
- **Time and energy management** — calendar audit, delegation, recovery.
- **Inclusive leadership** — recognizing bias, broadening the pool. See `dei-audit-guide`.

## Output format

Save the program design to `outputs/leadership-development/<program-name>.md` with:
- Audience and entry criteria.
- Curriculum (modules, duration, format).
- Roster (named participants if known).
- Facilitator(s) and budget.
- Success metrics (e.g., retention of new managers, promotion rates, NPS for the program).
- Cohort calendar.

For succession planning specifically, save `outputs/leadership-development/succession-plan.md` with named high-potentials, their development plans, and review cadence — store privately, not in a shared repo.

## Common mistakes

- One-size-fits-all program. First-time managers and senior directors need different things.
- Generic content. Use real situations from the org, not stock case studies.
- No follow-up. Training without practice + feedback fades within weeks.
- Treating leadership development as an HR check-box. Without exec sponsorship, it dies.
- No metrics. If you can't measure outcomes, the program will get cut in a downturn.
