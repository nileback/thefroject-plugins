# DEI Audit Guide

Assess where the organization stands on diversity, equity, and inclusion with data and structured analysis, not assumptions or aspirations.

## Input Required

1. **Workforce data** — demographics by level, department, tenure (check `context/current-data.md`)
2. **Policies** — hiring, promotion, compensation, leave, accommodation policies
3. **Survey data** — engagement surveys, inclusion surveys, exit interviews
4. **Process documentation** — hiring process, performance review process, promotion criteria
5. **Benchmark data** — industry demographics, peer company data (if available)

## Maturity Assessment

| Level | Definition | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| **1 — Compliance** | Meeting legal minimums only | Policies exist but are not actively enforced or promoted |
| **2 — Awareness** | Leadership acknowledges DEI importance | Training exists, diversity data collected, no targets |
| **3 — Commitment** | Active programs and investment | ERGs funded, DEI role exists, public goals set |
| **4 — Integration** | DEI embedded in business processes | Hiring, promotion, product decisions all consider DEI |
| **5 — Transformation** | DEI is a competitive advantage | Diverse leadership, inclusive culture, market differentiation |

## Principles

- Measure before you act. Initiatives without baseline data cannot demonstrate impact.
- Disaggregate the data. Company averages hide the experiences of underrepresented groups.
- Equity is not equality. Equal treatment of people in unequal positions perpetuates inequality. Equitable systems account for different starting points.
- This assessment identifies gaps. Addressing them requires sustained commitment, budget, and accountability, not a single initiative.
