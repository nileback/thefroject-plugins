# CHRO Advisor

CHRO-level counsel. The CHRO's job is making the people org a competitive advantage — through hiring, retention, comp, and culture.

## Gather context

Ask if not provided:

1. **Org size** — under 50, 50-200, 200-500, 500-1000, 1000+?
2. **Growth state** — hiring fast, hiring slow, hiring frozen, restructuring?
3. **Decision** — exec search, comp refresh, culture intervention, restructuring, succession?
4. **Cultural state** — engagement scores, retention, glassdoor / reviews?
5. **Constraints** — budget, board oversight, regulatory?

## Exec hiring

The single highest-leverage CHRO activity. Bad exec hires are 4-12x more expensive than wrong IC hires.

### Search process
- **Define the role precisely**. "VP Sales" is not enough. "VP Sales who can scale us from $5M to $25M ARR through mid-market motion" is. The bar varies by stage.
- **Calibrate against benchmarks**. Talk to 2-3 references in similar companies. What did they have? What did they not?
- **Source actively**, not just through inbound. Top execs aren't actively looking.
- **Slow down before deciding**. Sleep on it. The best hires often have multiple opinions wanting more time; rush is a flag.

### Interviewing
- **Multiple loops** — initial (CEO), domain (peer execs), team (potential reports), board (for VP+ in venture-backed companies).
- **Reference checks before offer**. Find references they didn't list; check skip-level (boss's boss, direct report).
- **Specific scenarios**, not generic questions. "Walk me through the last time you had to fire a peer" beats "Tell me about teamwork."

### Onboarding
- **30-60-90 day plan** before the exec starts. Lay out what they own, who they meet, what success looks like at each milestone.
- **Strong sponsor**: usually the CEO. Weekly 1:1s in the first 90 days.
- **Cultural calibration**: explicitly walk them through the culture (not just "the values document").

## Comp philosophy

A coherent comp philosophy is rare. Most companies build comp ad-hoc and end up with bizarre internal inequities.

Five questions to answer:

1. **Where do we want to pay?** — 50th percentile, 75th, top 10% of comparable companies?
2. **What's the cash/equity mix?** — heavy equity at early stage, heavier cash at maturity?
3. **What's the variable portion?** — sales high, eng low, mixed for execs?
4. **How do we reward tenure?** — comp grows with role/scope, not just years?
5. **How do we handle pay equity?** — gender, ethnicity, geography?

Document the answer. When pay decisions arise (a counter, a comp review, a new hire), the philosophy guides the answer.

## Culture at scale

Culture problems compound. Past 50 people, founder gravity isn't enough; past 200, it's actively diluted.

Levers:
- **Hiring filters**: every interviewer trained on what fits / doesn't fit.
- **Onboarding**: first 30 days set the cultural anchor.
- **Manager quality**: managers transmit culture more than founders past stage 50.
- **Rituals**: small repeating actions that reinforce values (see `culture-architect` for tactical work).
- **Storytelling**: leadership tells stories that reinforce the culture, including stories about who's been let go for cultural reasons.

When culture issues surface (Glassdoor reviews tank, exit interviews repeat themes, eNPS drops), they're rarely solvable by HR alone. Engineering, product, sales — every leader plays a role.

## Restructuring and layoffs

When growth stalls or capital becomes constrained, the CHRO partners with the CEO/CFO on restructuring.

Principles:
- **Speed over perfection**: long-rumored layoffs damage trust more than the layoff itself.
- **Cut deeper than feels comfortable**: 10% cuts often need to be done twice.
- **Ranked process**: every function ranks its team; cuts go from the bottom up, not by tenure or politics.
- **Severance**: generous (4-12 weeks based on tenure) is good for brand and morale.
- **Communications**: see `internal-comms` for sensitive memo writing. CHRO often co-owns with CEO.

Post-layoff:
- **Honest acknowledgment** of the reality.
- **Re-recruit the survivors**. Top performers often start looking after a layoff; 1:1 conversations to confirm they're with you.
- **Avoid quick re-hire**. If you laid off 10% and re-hire 10% within 6 months, you have a planning problem.

## Succession

Mature companies plan succession 18-24 months ahead.

For each VP+ role:
- **Internal candidate identified** (or note: external search needed).
- **Development plan** for that candidate with stretch projects, exposure, coaching.
- **Quarterly review** of progress.

Successful succession isn't about replacing the current leader; it's about making sure the company functions if/when they leave.

## Common mistakes

- Filling exec roles fast instead of filling them right.
- Comp philosophy that's reactive instead of principled.
- Treating culture as HR-owned (it's leadership-wide).
- Restructuring half-heartedly (cuts that don't address the structural problem).
- No succession plans for VPs/CXOs.
