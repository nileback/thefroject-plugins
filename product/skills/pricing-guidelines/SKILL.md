# Pricing Guidelines

This skill is a knowledge layer. Commands that produce proposals, SOWs, or pricing pages read this before presenting any pricing.

## How To Use This File

Fill in your actual tiers, rates, and models below. Every command that touches pricing reads this file first, so pricing information stays consistent across all documents.

## Engagement Models

Define your standard engagement types:

| Model | Structure | Best for | Billing |
|-------|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Project-based** | Fixed scope, fixed price, defined deliverables | Clear requirements, defined end state | Milestone-based or 50/50 |
| **Retainer** | Monthly hours or output commitment | Ongoing work, evolving scope | Monthly recurring |
| **Advisory** | Strategic guidance, lighter touch | Senior leadership, strategic decisions | Monthly or quarterly |
| **Hybrid** | Retainer base + project add-ons | Long-term relationships with periodic projects | Base monthly + milestone |

### Model Selection Guide

| Client signal | Recommended model |
|---------------|-------------------|
| "We need X built by Y date" | Project-based |
| "We need ongoing support" | Retainer |
| "We need help figuring out what to do" | Advisory |
| "We need a partner, not a vendor" | Hybrid |
| "We have a small budget but ongoing needs" | Retainer (smaller scope) |

## Pricing Tiers

Customize this table with your actual pricing:

| Tier | Description | Starting at | Typical scope | Target client |
|------|------------|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| Starter | Single workstream | $X/month | 1 deliverable type | Small teams, testing the relationship |
| Growth | Multi-workstream | $X/month | 2-3 deliverable types | Scaling teams, proven need |
| Enterprise | Full partnership | Custom | Full-service engagement | Large orgs, strategic accounts |

### Tier Design Principles

- Each tier serves a distinct buyer persona (not just "more of the same")
- The gap between tiers should feel meaningful, not arbitrary
- Every tier has a clear reason to exist and a clear reason to upgrade
- The middle tier should be the most popular (design it that way)

## Pricing Copy Rules

### Presenting the Number

1. **Show value before price.** List what is included, what it delivers, and what changes for the client. Then present the investment.
2. **Anchor high.** Present the highest tier or full engagement first. Then show the option that fits. The first number sets the reference point.
3. **Use "investment" not "cost."** Cost implies expense. Investment implies return.
4. **Always include scope.** Never present a price without: deliverables, timeline, and what is NOT included.

### Framing Patterns

| Pattern | When to use | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **ROI frame** | Client is ROI-focused | "For $X/month, you get Y, which saves $Z" |
| **Comparison frame** | Client is evaluating alternatives | "Comparable agencies charge $X. Our rate is $Y with more senior talent." |
| **Per-unit frame** | Large numbers feel intimidating | "$X per user per month" instead of "$120,000/year" |
| **Payback frame** | Long sales cycles | "Pays for itself in 3 months based on current metrics" |

### What NOT to Include in Pricing Sections

- Hourly rates (unless the client specifically asks). Frame as outcomes, not hours.
- Disclaimers that undermine confidence ("prices subject to change")
- Complex matrices with dozens of line items (simplify)
- Discounts without explanation (signals low confidence in pricing)

## Discount Policy

| Situation | Acceptable discount | What you get in return |
|-----------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Annual commitment | 10-15% | Cash flow predictability |
| Case study rights | 5-10% | Marketing asset |
| Referral to qualified lead | 5-10% (one-time) | Pipeline growth |
| Beta/pilot | 15-25% | Feedback and proof point |
| Strategic account | Negotiate | Long-term revenue and brand association |

**Rules:**
- Never discount without getting something in return
- Never discount more than 25% (signals pricing is inflated)
- Document all non-standard pricing in the SOW
- Discounts expire. Include a date.

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response framework |
|-----------|--------------------|
| "Too expensive" | Reframe around ROI. "What is the cost of NOT doing this for another quarter?" |
| "Competitor is cheaper" | "What are they including at that price?" (often less scope) |
| "Can we start smaller?" | Offer the Starter tier with a clear upgrade path |
| "We need to think about it" | "What specific information would help you decide?" |
| "Can you do it for $X?" | "At that budget, here is what we can deliver. Would that solve your core problem?" |

## Do NOT
- Present pricing without context (scope, timeline, deliverables)
- Discount without getting something in return
- Show hourly rates when you can frame as outcomes
- Present a single option. Always present 2-3 tiers or options.
- Mix pricing models in the same proposal without explaining why
- Forget to specify what is NOT included (scope creep prevention)
