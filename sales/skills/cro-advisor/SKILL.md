# CRO Advisor

CRO-level counsel covering revenue strategy across new business, expansion, and retention.

## Gather context

Ask if not provided:

1. **Sales motion** — SMB, mid-market, enterprise, hybrid?
2. **Current revenue scale** — $1M-$10M, $10M-$50M, $50M+ ARR?
3. **Org state** — number of reps, roles (SDR, AE, AM, CSM), tenure mix?
4. **Decision** — segmentation, comp plan, hiring plan, forecast discipline, expansion focus?
5. **Pain** — what triggered the conversation? (Quota miss, attrition, leaky funnel, expansion plateau)

## Revenue org structure

Stage-appropriate structure:

### $1M-$5M ARR
- Founders or 1-2 AEs handling everything.
- No SDRs; all leads come through inbound or founder-led.
- No CSMs; AEs handle post-sale.

### $5M-$15M ARR
- 3-8 AEs.
- 1-3 SDRs (or BDRs) feeding pipeline.
- 1-2 CSMs handling onboarding + renewals.
- Sales engineering function emerging (could be a role or a partner).
- Revenue ops function: 1 person owning systems + reporting.

### $15M-$50M ARR
- Segmented AE teams (SMB / mid-market).
- SDR pod per segment.
- Dedicated AMs separate from CSMs (renewals + expansion).
- Sales engineering function with 2-4 SEs.
- Rev ops with manager + analyst.

### $50M+
- Segmented further (enterprise, mid-market, SMB).
- Geographic specialization possible.
- Industry verticalization possible.
- Enablement function with dedicated team.
- Sales operations + revenue operations potentially split.

Don't over-build org structure ahead of revenue. Specialization that's not utilized creates overhead and bureaucracy.

## Compensation design principles

Comp plan failure modes are some of the most expensive mistakes in revenue.

### Foundations
- **Base / variable split** — typical 60/40 (sales-led) or 50/50 (transactional). PLG-led teams skew lower variable.
- **Quota / OTE multiplier** — typical 4-5x (rep generates 4-5x their OTE in revenue). Lower means inefficient; higher signals quotas may be hard.
- **Pay frequency** — monthly, quarterly. Quarterly is most common; aligns with sales cycle.

### Mechanics
- **Accelerators** — pay 1.5x or 2x for revenue above 100% quota. Drives top performance.
- **Decelerators** — fractional pay below ~70-80% quota; threshold ensures effort isn't wasted on near-miss deals.
- **Spiffs** — short-term bonuses for specific behaviors (new product launch, target segment).
- **Multi-year deals** — pay on TCV with claw-back protections, or pay annually as ARR is recognized.
- **Commission caps** — usually a mistake; caps top performers and signals limited upside.

### Watchouts
- **Mid-year plan changes** — destroy trust and motivation. Avoid except in extreme cases.
- **Team quotas** — undermine individual accountability; use only when truly collaborative work warrants.
- **Single-source comp** — relying entirely on revenue can incentivize short-term deals over long-term value. Mix in retention or expansion components for AMs.

## Segmentation strategy

Most companies under-segment. Different ICPs respond to different motions.

Segmentation dimensions:
- **Company size** — SMB, mid-market, enterprise. Most common axis.
- **Industry / vertical** — when the product fit varies meaningfully by industry.
- **Geography** — when sales motion or buying behavior differs (e.g., EMEA vs Americas).
- **Use case** — when the product serves multiple distinct use cases.

Don't segment until you have evidence the motion differs. Segmentation adds overhead.

## Forecasting discipline

CRO owns the revenue forecast to leadership and the board. Methodology:

- **Bottom-up** — each rep submits their commit/best/worst per deal.
- **Top-down** — historical conversion rates × current pipeline.
- **Reconciled** — leadership compares both, identifies gaps.

The CRO's job is rejecting "happy ears" and forcing realism. Reps and managers consistently optimistic = forecast too high; chronic 80% attainment vs. forecast tells you something is wrong with the discipline, not the team.

## Expansion vs. new business

Mature revenue orgs balance both:
- **New business** drives top-line growth.
- **Expansion** is typically more efficient (lower CAC, higher margin).
- **Net revenue retention** > 110% is the holy grail; > 130% is best-in-class.

CRO's strategic question: how much of next year's growth comes from expansion vs new business? Most B2B SaaS companies under-invest in expansion at growth stage.

## Common mistakes

- Over-segmenting before evidence justifies.
- Specialization (SDRs, AEs, CSMs) too early when generalists would suffice.
- Comp plan changes mid-year.
- Optimistic forecasting tolerated quarter after quarter.
- Under-investing in expansion at the cost of top-line.
