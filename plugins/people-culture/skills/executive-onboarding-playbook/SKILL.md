# Executive Onboarding Playbook

Onboard a new executive (VP, SVP, C-level) into the role. The first 90 days set the trajectory; bad onboarding costs 12-24 months and a likely re-hire.

## Gather context

Ask if not provided:

1. **Role** — VP Sales, VP Eng, CFO, etc.?
2. **Hiring manager** — CEO directly? Another exec?
3. **Why this role?** — what triggered the hire (gap, growth, replacement)?
4. **Team they inherit** — direct reports, size, history?
5. **Predecessor** — was there one? Why did they leave?
6. **Current state** — what's working, what's broken in the function?

## Pre-start (week -2 to 0)

Before the exec walks in:

- **Communicate the hire** — internal announcement to the team they'll lead, public where appropriate.
- **Set up access** — laptop, email, all systems day-one ready.
- **Prep the meet-list** — first 4 weeks of 1:1s scheduled.
- **Document the current state** — give them a "state of the function" memo from CEO + key team members. 5-10 pages, concrete.
- **Define success** — written 30-60-90 day expectations. Don't make them guess.

## Week 1: Land

Focus: meet the people, understand the work.

- **CEO 1:1** day 1, 30 min — set tone, expectations, comms cadence.
- **Direct reports 1:1s** — 60 min each in week 1 if possible. Standard questions: what's working, what's not, what would you change, what's at risk.
- **Skip-level conversations** with 2-3 layers down — don't make all input go through direct reports.
- **Cross-functional peer 1:1s** with other VPs/CXOs — set tone for collaboration.
- **Customer call shadowing** (3-5 calls) for any role facing customers.
- **No major decisions**. Listen mode.

End of week 1: a written summary of "what I'm hearing" — informs the next 60 days.

## Weeks 2-4: Diagnose

Focus: understand the function deeply enough to plan.

- **Continue 1:1s and skip-levels**.
- **Read everything**: function strategy docs, OKRs, dashboards, post-mortems, key contracts.
- **Sit in on operating meetings** — leadership, function-level, customer-facing as appropriate.
- **External calibration**: call peers in similar roles at other companies. Compare notes. Identify gaps.

End of week 4: a written diagnosis to the CEO. Key sections:
- Current state assessment (honest).
- Top 3 strengths to build on.
- Top 3 gaps or risks.
- Recommended priorities for the next 60 days.
- Resourcing implications.

## Days 30-60: Plan + early wins

Focus: align on direction, ship visible early wins.

- **CEO alignment** on priorities and resource asks.
- **Team alignment** — present the 90-day plan to direct reports; get input.
- **Pick 1-2 early wins** — small, visible, completed by day 60. Builds credibility internally.
- **Identify keepers and leavers**. Day 60 isn't when to make people changes (too early), but the exec should have a view.

## Days 60-90: Establish + decide

Focus: settle in, make the harder decisions.

- **Operating cadence**: confirm weekly/monthly/quarterly rhythm with the team.
- **Goals + metrics** for the next quarter, set by the exec with team input.
- **First hires / fires** if needed — usually best done day 60-90 once fully oriented.
- **Strategic point of view**: present a longer-term direction (1-2 year horizon) to CEO and leadership team.

End of 90 days: the exec is operating. CEO can step out of the close support mode (though weekly 1:1s continue).

## CEO partnership through the 90 days

The CEO's role is making the exec successful, not testing them.

- **Weekly 1:1** for the first 90 days; minimum.
- **Air cover**: protect them from miscellaneous demands so they can land.
- **Honest feedback** — if something's off-track, say so early. Don't let issues fester.
- **Defend their decisions** publicly when the exec makes calls others disagree with.
- **Identify patterns**: if peer execs are pushing back on the new hire, dig into why.

## When onboarding goes wrong

Warning signs (act on these, don't wait):
- 30 days in: exec hasn't formed clear views, can't articulate function priorities.
- 60 days in: team not engaged with new exec; complaints to CEO/HR.
- 90 days in: cross-functional peers don't trust the exec's judgment.

These are recoverable but signal urgent intervention. The longer you wait, the harder the recovery.

## Output format

For the exec to use during their own onboarding: `outputs/exec-onboarding/<exec-name>-<role>/` folder with subfolders by week:
- `week-1/` — meeting notes, what-I'm-hearing summary.
- `weeks-2-4/` — diagnosis memo.
- `days-30-60/` — 90-day plan.
- `days-60-90/` — operating cadence, goals, strategic point of view.

For the CEO supporting: a checklist at `outputs/exec-onboarding/<exec-name>-ceo-checklist.md` tracking weekly support.

## Common mistakes

- No documented expectations. Exec guesses; CEO surprised.
- Week 1 = jump straight into decisions. Doesn't work.
- 90-day plan that's a recycled job description. Should be specific and earned through diagnosis.
- CEO disengaged. Worst-case onboarding scenario.
- Treating exec as a peer immediately. They earn that through their first 90 days.
